Why J.C.

The difference between
a speaker and a witness.

There are thousands of resilience speakers in the UK. Most of them are very good. They have read the books, completed the certifications, and can deliver a polished 45-minute talk about overcoming adversity.

J.C. is not a resilience speaker. He is a man who survived something most people will never face — and who rebuilt a career, a family, and a purpose from the wreckage. The difference in the room is immediate. People do not applaud out of politeness. They go quiet, because they recognise something real.

Combine that with 20+ years of operational leadership and a current board-level role — and you have a speaker who can move an audience emotionally and challenge them strategically in the same hour.

01
Genuinely lived it
Not a near-miss. Not a business setback. A prison sentence, a complete collapse, and a rebuild from zero — while maintaining a marriage, a family, and ultimately a board seat.
02
Still in the arena
Operations Director at Marshall Tufflex. Board member. Author. Coach. J.C. is not speaking from retirement — he is speaking from the front line.
03
Leaves people changed
The measure of a great speaker is not the applause at the end — it is the conversation that happens afterwards. J.C. starts conversations that continue for months.

Topics

Five talks.
One thread.

Each topic can be delivered as a standalone keynote or combined into a half-day workshop. All are drawn from real lived experience — not research, not theory, not other people's stories.

01
Resilience Under Extreme Pressure
What resilience actually looks like when the stakes are real — not in a survival workshop, but in a prison cell at 5am with nothing left. The framework J.C. built from the inside, and how it applies to any leadership crisis.
Keynote · Workshop
02
Rebuilding Identity After Failure
The hardest leadership journey is not the climb — it is the rebuild after a complete collapse. How to reconstruct your sense of self, your purpose, and your authority when everything you thought you were has been stripped away.
Keynote · Workshop
03
Strategic Clarity When the Stakes Are Real
How the engineer's mind applies to leadership under crisis. When everything is noise, how do you find the signal? A practical talk on decision-making, prioritisation and presence under extreme pressure.
Keynote · Corporate
04
The Unconventional Leader
Why the leaders who break the rules outperform the ones who follow them. What prison taught J.C. about authority, influence, and what real leadership looks like when there are no titles, no budgets, and no safety net.
Keynote · Leadership
05
From Failure to Fuel
How to turn the worst experience of your life into your sharpest competitive edge. The counterintuitive truth about setback, shame, and the kind of purpose that can only be found on the other side of losing everything.
Keynote · Closing

Formats

Built around your event.

Keynote

45–60 minutes. A complete narrative arc from the first word to the last. Designed to open or close a conference — or stand alone as the centrepiece of your event.

45–60 min · In-person or virtual

Workshop

Half-day or full-day immersive session combining narrative with practical frameworks. Groups of up to 30 leaders. Leaves participants with tools they can use the next day.

Half-day or full-day · Max 30 people

Panel & Fireside

Panel participation or intimate fireside conversation format. Particularly powerful for leadership development days and private gatherings where direct, unscripted dialogue is the goal.

60–90 min · Any size

Corporate Day

Full day · Leadership teams

Private Gathering

Virtual Keynote

45–60 min · Global availability
